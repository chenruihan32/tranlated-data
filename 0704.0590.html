I Introduction

Algebraic-Geometric (AG) codes [1] offer desirable properties such as large code lengths over small finite fields, the potential to find a large selection of codes and good error-correction at high code rates [2]. In recent years, an important class of one-point AG codes, called Hermitian codes, has been frequently discussed [3]-[6].

For a Hermitian code defined over Gâ€‹Fâ€‹(q2)ğºğ¹superscriptğ‘2GF(q^{2}), a brute-force way to design an encoder is to multiply the information vector by a generator matrix. The space complexity of a serial-in serial-out architecture for this systematic encoder is Oâ€‹(q5)ğ‘‚superscriptğ‘5O(q^{5}) in terms of finite field multipliers and Oâ€‹(q3)ğ‘‚superscriptğ‘3O(q^{3}) in terms of memory elements. The encoder requires 2â€‹n2ğ‘›2n clock cycles to generate a codeword of length nğ‘›n, thus, the latency is nğ‘›n.

By considering a Hermitian code as a superposition of several generalized Reed Solomon (RS) codes, an encoding scheme is introduced in [4]. In [5], an encoding algorithm by forming a bivariate information polynomial and evaluating this polynomial at every finite rational point on the Hermitian curve is proposed. However, both such schemes are nonsystematic and involve the evaluation of bivariate polynomials at nğ‘›n finite rational points, thus, they may not have efficient hardware architecture for implementations.

A computationally efficient approach for systematic encoding was proposed in [7]. A serial-in serial-out architecture for this approach was proposed in [8]. This architecture requires nğ‘›n clock cycles to encode a codeword of length nğ‘›n. The space complexity for this architecture is Oâ€‹(q3)ğ‘‚superscriptğ‘3O(q^{3}), both in terms of finite field multipliers and memory elements.

In this paper, we present an algorithm for systematic encoding and syndrome computation of Hermitian codes. We give an outline for the encoder architecture, which uses qğ‘q varying-rate RS encoders as main blocks and requires n2/3superscriptğ‘›23n^{2/3} clock cycles for encoding a codeword of length nğ‘›n. The space complexity of the architecture is Oâ€‹(q2)ğ‘‚superscriptğ‘2O(q^{2}) in terms of both, memory elements and finite field multipliers.

A low complexity algorithm for systematic encoding and syndrome computation of Hermitian codes has been presented.
