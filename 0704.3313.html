5 Conclusion and Future Directions

In this paper, we study the straggler identification problem for data streams, showing that small sublinear-space indexing schemes exist for performing straggler detection. Another way of viewing this problem is that we desire a database indexing scheme that can represent a dynamic set using a compact structure, Dğ·D. As the database Dğ·D fills to be of size as large as nğ‘›n, the cells of Dğ·D can â€œoverflowâ€ and we lose the ability to list the contents of Dğ·D. But as items are removed from Dğ·D, we eventually get to a point where we can enumerate the contents of Dğ·D again.

Our deterministic solution uses Oâ€‹(dâ€‹logâ¡n)ğ‘‚ğ‘‘ğ‘›O(d\log n) bits to represent Dğ·D, where dğ‘‘d is a parameter indicating an upper bound on the number of stragglers we expect to exist at the time when we wish to enumerate the contents of Dğ·D. We observe that this deterministic solution cannot tolerate redundant insertions or false deletions, but this requirement is justified by our negativity result for any deterministic solution to the straggler identification problem. Our randomize solution, on the hand, which introduces the invertible Bloom filter, can tolerate both redundant insertions and false deletions, provided there are not too many of them.

In all our solutions, we assume we have an upper bound, dğ‘‘d, on the size of Dğ·D at the time we wish to perform enumerations of its contents. One direction of future study, then, is to reduce this requirement of knowledge of an upper bound dğ‘‘d, for example, for insertion-deletion sequences that belong to certain probabilistic distributions.
