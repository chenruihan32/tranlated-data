The Complexity of HCP in Digraps with Degree Bound Two

Guohun Zhu
Abstract

The Hamiltonian cycle problem (HCP) in digraphs D𝐷D with degree bound two is solved by two mappings in this paper. The first bijection is between an incidence matrix Cn​msubscript𝐶𝑛𝑚C_{nm} of simple digraph and an incidence matrix F𝐹F of balanced bipartite undirected graph G𝐺G; The second mapping is from a perfect matching of G𝐺G to a cycle of D𝐷D. It proves that the complexity of HCP in D𝐷D is polynomial, and finding a second non-isomorphism Hamiltonian cycle from a given Hamiltonian digraph with degree bound two is also polynomial. Lastly it deduces P=N​P𝑃𝑁𝑃P=NP base on the results.

As the result of [2], the complexity of HCP in digraph with bound two is N​P−c​o​m​p​l​e​t​e𝑁𝑃𝑐𝑜𝑚𝑝𝑙𝑒𝑡𝑒NP-complete. According the theorem 5.1, the complexity of HCP in digraph with bound two is also P𝑃P, thus according to proposition 2, P=N​P𝑃𝑁𝑃P=NP.

7 Conclusion

According to the theorem 5.1, the complexity of determining a Hamiltonian cycle existence or not in digraph with bound degree two is in polynomial time. And according to the theorem 6.1, P𝑃P versus N​P𝑁𝑃NP problem has closed, P=N​P𝑃𝑁𝑃P=NP.
