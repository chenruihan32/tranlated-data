Reducing SAT to 2-SAT

Sergey Gubin
Abstract

Description of a polynomial time reduction of SAT to 2-SAT of polynomial size.

1 Introduction

Among all dimensions, 2-SAT possesses many special properties unique in the sense of computational complexity [2, 3, 4, 5, 6]. But in light of works [7, 9, 8, 10] a problem arose: either those properties are accidental or there are polynomial time reductions of SAT to 2-SAT of polynomial size. This article describes one such reduction.

4 SAT vs. 1-SAT

Let’s take one step further. Applying to formula (1)/(5) either of the variations of the compatibility matrices method [7] will produce a Boolean matrix. Let it be a matrix R𝑅R:

R=(ri​j)a×b.𝑅subscriptsubscript𝑟𝑖𝑗𝑎𝑏R=(r_{ij})_{a\times b}.

Size of the matrix depends on the method’s variation and the order of clauses in formula (1). The size can be changed if permute the clauses and repeat the method [7]. The formula (1) is satisfiable iff matrix R𝑅R contains t​r​u​e𝑡𝑟𝑢𝑒true-elements [7] (elements which are t​r​u​e𝑡𝑟𝑢𝑒true). The existence/absence of the t​r​u​e𝑡𝑟𝑢𝑒true-elements is the only invariant.
If formula (1) is unsatisfiable, then that formula is reducible to formula “f​a​l​s​e𝑓𝑎𝑙𝑠𝑒false”. Otherwise, formula (1) is reducible to a 1-SAT instance.

Let’s permute all clauses and make those shortest clauses to be the last ones in formula (1). Then, result of the modified method [7] will be a matrix R𝑅R of size less than 3×3333\times 3. That proves the following theorem.

 Theorem 1.

Any SAT instance is reducible to a 1-SAT instance with 999 variables or less. A SAT instance is unsatisfiable iff its 1-SAT presentation is “f​a​l​s​e𝑓𝑎𝑙𝑠𝑒false” - there is not any variables in its 1-SAT presentation.

5 Conclusions

Formula (1) may be thought of as a “Business Requirements”. And any appropriate computer program may be thought of as a solution of the SAT instance. Then, theorem 1 can be an explanation of the remarkable efficiency of the “natural programs”. From this point of view, the iterations of the method of compatibility matrices may be thought of as a learning/modeling of the business domain. In the artificial programming, the calculation of the compatibility matrices - a virtual business domain - could be a conclusion of the stage “Business Requirements Analysis/Mathematical Modeling”. That would improve the programs’ performance. The resulting compatibility matrices may be thought of as a fussy logic’s tables of rules for the domain.
The whole solution of formula (1) can be achieved, with one of the following approaches, for example. ANN approach is the applying of the compatibility matrices method backward, starting from matrix R𝑅R. An example of that can be found in [8]. DTM approach is the looping trough of the following three steps: selection of any t​r​u​e𝑡𝑟𝑢𝑒true-element from matrix R𝑅R; substitution of the appropriate t​r​u​e𝑡𝑟𝑢𝑒true-assignments in formula (1); and repeating of the compatibility matrices method. The last method is an implication of the self-reducibility property of SAT [6].
In certain sense, theorem 1 may be seen as an answer to the Feasibility Thesis [3].
